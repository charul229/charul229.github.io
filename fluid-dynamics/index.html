In Stokes' first problem , fluid is driven by suddenly accelerating plate and in Stokes' second problem, plate is oschillated with a certain frequency. Second problem is the simplest unsteady problem which has analytical solution of Navier-stokes equation. Stokes' wave theory is also important to understand. Solitary wave which is also called as soliton is a unique wave which has distinct properties from other waves first observed by Russel. Other scientist did not agree as they could not figure it out. Later KDv equation is developed which provided the solution. Inverse scattering method is used to solve non-linear partial differential equation.

Pyroclastic flows: A pyroclastic flow is a fast-moving current of hot gas and volcanic matter that flows along the ground away from a volcano at average speeds of 100 km/h but is capable of reaching speeds up to 700 km/h. Chute flows: steep sided flow, Debris flows, Granular flows in a rotating drum.

Diiferent atmospheric layers in the outer earth, troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, thermophere and exosphere and last that is magneto sphere. Troposphere is the layer where our most of the air and moisture stays, cloud and turbulence in flows stays. Here the temperature decreases with height. In stratosphere, ozone layer exist. It absorbs ultrasonic radiations from the sun so the temperature increases in the layer as height increases. Mesosphere again shows decrease in temperature. Thermosphere is an important layer which is also called ionosphere because here ions forms because of radiations from son, here temperature increases with height. This layer casues the radio transmission possible on our planet.
