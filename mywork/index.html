Local similarity solution and their limitation_Moffat_1979

This is a theoretical construction.

Effect of inertia in a forced corner flows_Moffat_1981

In this paper, boundary conditions are considered over a solid surface and on a liquid surface.

Hydrodynamc model of steady movement of solid-liquid-fluid system contact line_JCIS_1971_Huh & Scriven

This is the first paper which explains the dynamics of moving contact line in two phases. The model aims to clarify that no-slip boundary condition causes non-integrable singularity at the contact line. Their study reports two parameters namely viscosity ratio and dynamic contact angle to be flow characterizing parameters. The key inference from the theory was that more viscous fluid largely shows a rolling motion near the moving contact line.

On the motion of fluid-fluid interface along the solid surface_Dussan_1974_JFM

Dussan and Davis, 1974,JFM, examined the kinemtaics of moving contact lines in general and showed that contact line motion is impossible if no-slip condition holds and showed that drop rolls along the solid.

The physics of moving wetting line_Blake_2006_Blake

This paper explains the theoretical models developed till that data do not precisely predict the dynamics occurring near the moving contact line. There is a second approach to spreading which involves zero bulk flow of the liquid but instead postulates that molecules from the liquid near the contact-line jump across and attach to the substrate effectively moving the contact line forward; this gives the molecular kinetic theory (MKT). HDT and MKT... HDT works for free spreading of drop as there are only natural time scales are invloved in the problem but in forced spreading case there is one more time scale involves which is related to relaxation which causes the dynamics to be different from free spreading case. For forced spreading case, MKT works well.

Forced versus spontaneous spreading of liquids_Davis_langmuir_2013

This paper take two different systems on for droplet spreading over a solid surface and another system of plate-advancing experiments.

Forced wetting and Hydrodynamic assist_PoF_Blake2015

This paper explains the mechanism of hydrodynamic assist which helps to prevent the air entrainment in advancing contact line motion. It delays the wetting failure by increasing the critical speed at which failure occurs. It is simply the degree to which the flow influences the dynamic contact angle that increases during forced wetting.

Hydrodynamic theory of forced dewetting_Eggers_2004_PRL
A Characteristic lengths at moving contact lines for a perfectly wetting fluid: the influence of speed on the dynamic contact angle. J. Fluid Mech. 1999

Eggers and stone have shown that Lmac/Lmic is a velocity dependent parameter. (A Characteristic lengths at moving contact lines for a perfectly wetting fluid: the influence of speed on the dynamic contact angle. J. Fluid Mech. 1999). They obtained the dependence of (Lmac/Lmic = a Ca^(2/3)) on the capillary number for perfectly wetting liquids. They assumed a nearly flat interface near contact line ato apply the lubrication assumption for describing liquid motion. They have considered the pressure variation caused by capillary and vanderWaals forces.

On air entrainment in coatings_JCIS_2003_Simpkins

This paper focus on advancing moving contact line motion. The entrainment happens when the contact angle becomes 180 degree.

Hydrodynamic theory if liquid slippage on a solid substrate near a moving contact line_Davis_2013_PRL

This paper reports a slip model which is a spatially varying along the solid surface near a moving contact line. After empploying biharmonic governing equation and standard boundary equation, they came up with secular equation and using friction and shear stress balance near the contact point, they made it a well posed problem. They ultimately obtained MKT solution with formulation.

Moffatt votices induced by the motion of a contact line_Rapid_JFM_2014_Davis

This is an extension of earlier study of liquid slippage near a contact line in 2013 PRL. By involving the same slip model, they reported flow fields near a contact line. They claimed that if n is complex then moffatt vorticed might be observed near the moving contact line. A number of questions are pointed out on the complex values of n and other model pararmeter b and r_star which are important in the study of moving contact line. r_star which is a real distance over a solid surface coming up complex which limits the model to keep the n value to be real only.

Existence of Moffatt votices near a moving contact line between two fluids_PRF_2017_Febres

The study reports solution for two phase fluid system in the inner region near a moving contact line. Basically, this is an extension of the single phase study of Kirkinis and Davis 2014. The study reports the flow fields with different values of n namely, complex values and real values. When real values of n are considered then Moffatt vortices got disappeared and rolling motion and split streamline motion can be observed.

Movement of contact line in gas-liquid-solid phase systems_2016_Gandhi

He talked about the no slip boundary condition near a moving contact line.

Microscopic and macroscopic dynamic interface shapes and interpretation of dynamic contact angles_Rame_Garoff_1995

This study provides the interface shapes comparison with static interface shape.

Dynamics of spreading of liquids on a solid surface. Part_1 viscous flows_Cox_1986_JFM

This paper employs constant slip near a moving contact line. The region near a contact line is divided into three namely, inner region which is slip dominated, outer region which is geometry dependent and intermediate region which bridges the two regions. The study relates the dynamic contact angle to the capillary length and slip length which further modified to predict the interface shape. The idea of stagnation point is also mentioned.

Inertial and viscous effects on dynamic contact angle_Cox_1998_JFM

The governing equation involves the inertial terms and divide the region into two namely, one which is viscous dominated and second which is potentioal flow.

Velocity fields near a moving contact line_Chen_1997_JFM

This study combines the interface shape study with the flow fields for obtuse contact angle case with very small Ca.
